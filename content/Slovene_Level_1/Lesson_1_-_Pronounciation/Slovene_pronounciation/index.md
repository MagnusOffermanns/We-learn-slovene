After learning the different letters of the slovene Alphabet we will dive into the pronounciation of slovene words. What we will try to do is to give you an overview over how to pronounce each letter, with an example word. I will try to provide the [IPA pronounciation code](https://www.vocabulary.com/resources/ipa-pronunciation/) of each word but for everyone who is not fluent in this very mistirious letters I will also provide a audio via [forvo.com](https://forvo.com) so that one can also listen to its pronounciation.

As the pronounciation rules of slovene are rather complex I want to give you an applied overview over the pronounciation. I will give you each letter and a couple of examples how to pronounce them letter in different contexts. In this way we also learn some usefull frases that we can then use when strolling through the strees of Ljubljana or walk the medows of V√©lika plan√≠na.

(Don't worry, the hard ones like `ƒç`,`≈°`,`v` and `l` are getting some extra explanation üòã)

In the end I will provide some anki decks so that you can train the pronounciation of some slovene words and sentences by yourselfs and can train to apply the rules we have looked into in this chapter.

Lets start!

## Pronounciation of vouwels

First we will look at the vouwels i.e. `a`,`e`,`i`,`o`,`u`.

- `A`
  
  The `a` can be pronounced in a long way like in the word "far" **long* an example is the word `mati` mother [üì¢](https://forvo.com/word/mati/#sl).

  The second way `a` can be pronounce is like the a in "above" **short** that sounds like (uh) is `rama` which means shoulder [üì¢](https://forvo.com/word/rama/#sl)

- `E`

    The 'e' can also be pronounced in two ways, the first beeing pronounced **long** in `bled` (ehh) [üì¢](https://forvo.com/word/energija/#sl)

    The second way to pronounce the letter 'e' is **short** like in the english word `met` (eh) "energija" (energy) [üì¢](https://forvo.com/word/energija/#sl)

- `I`
  
    Also the I can be pronounced in two ways, short and long. The first way to pronounce i is like the english "ee" in see one exaple in slovene would be `vino` (wine)   [üì¢](https://forvo.com/word/vino/#sl)

    The second way to pronounce the "i" is short like in the english word "bit" (ih) `list` ( leaf / sheet ) [üì¢](https://forvo.com/word/list/#sl).

- `O`
    The we have the `O` that can also be pronounced long or short. Lets give two examples:
  - **Long** like the 'oa' in "cloak" one example slovene word would be `most` which means "bridge" [üì¢](https://forvo.com/word/most/#sl)
  - **Short** like the i in the english word "bit" one slovene word would be `moka` which means flour [üì¢](https://forvo.com/word/ajdova_moka/#sl)

>[!INFO] Znani mostovi Ljubljane (Famouse Bridges of Slovenia)
> Ljubljana has two famouse bridges, the [dragon bridge](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dragon_Bridge_(Ljubljana)) (Zmajski most) which is most likely the most famouse one for tourist because it has 4 beautiful dragon statues. Another beautiful bridge, known for its uniqueness is [Tromostovje](https://sl.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tromostovje) (which means three bridges) . It is the innoficial center of Ljubljana and is one bridge consisting of three sub-bridges. It is now a beautiful pedestrian area, while just a couple of years ago cars were still able to go over it (exactly till September 2007), which is now unimaginable for all inhabitants of Slovenia. Both bridges go over the Ljubljanica, the river (`reka`) flowing through the capital of Slovenia.

- `U`
    The letter `U` can also be short and long:
  - **Long** like in the english word pool - An slovene example would be 'guma' (rubber or tire) [üì¢](https://forvo.com/word/guma/#sl)
  - **Short** like in the english word book - An slovene example would be 'ura' [üì¢](https://forvo.com/word/ura/#sl)

## Pronounciation of consonants

Now we will look into consonants. The ones we are looking at here are easy to pronounce. The difficult ones get a extra chapter where we will also give you some examples to train the pronounciation.

- `B`

  The b is pronounced like the `b` in "best" [end of a word 'p' sound]; `bolha` (flea)

  >[!Note] Bolha.com
  The slovene craigslist is named after the flea (maybe because it is a a digital fleamarket?). Under [www.bolha.com](https://www.bolha.com/) one can find everything the slovene second hand enthusiast desires.

- `C`

  It is pronounced like the 'ts' in "bets". An example slovene word is `car` pronounced (tsar) [üì¢](https://forvo.com/word/car/#sl) which means king.

- `ƒå`

  It is pronounced like the 'ch' in the english term "chocolate". A slovene word is "ƒçmrlj" (bumble-bee [üì¢](https://forvo.com/search/%C4%8Dmrlj/)) 

- `D`

  The `d` can be pronounced in two ways:
  1. First it is pronounced like the 'd' in "day" if it is in the middle of a sentence. An example slovene word is `dan` which means day [üì¢](https://forvo.com/word/dan/#sl).
  2. If it is in the end of a sentence it is pronounced like a t in cat. One example is the word `led` which means ice [üì¢](https://forvo.com/word/led/#sl). 

- `F`

  The 'f' is pronounced like in "far". An example would be the word  `fi≈æol` which means [üì¢](https://forvo.com/word/fi%C5%BEol/#sl).

- `G`

  The `g` can be pronounced in two ways:
  1. If it is in the middle of a word it is pronounced like  in "go" one example is the word `galeb` [üì¢](https://forvo.com/word/galeb/#sl)
  2. If it occurs at the end of a word it sounds like a 'k' in the word 'rock' like an examplatory slovene word is `rog` which means 'horn'. [üì¢](https://forvo.com/word/ko%C4%8Devski_rog/#sl)

  >[!Note] Legends of Slovenia
  A famouse legendary animal of slovenia is the `zlato rog` a magical goat with golden horns. It roams the triglav national park and shows himself to only the most lucky of hickers. It is on the cover of the slovenian beer La≈°ko.

- `H`
  
  The 'h' is pronounced like the first h in "hannukkah" in spanish ([üì¢](https://forvo.com/search/Hanukkah/es/)) one slovene example would be the  `hajduk split` (The bandits of Split, the basketball club of Split, a city in Croatia) [üì¢](https://forvo.com/word/hajduk_split/#hr)

- `J`

  The J is pronounced like the the 'y' in "Yankee", one example would be the word `jabolko` which means Apple [üì¢](https://forvo.com/word/jabolko/#sl).

- `K`

  The the 'k' is pronounced like in the english word "kick". An example word in slovene is  `kava` the word for the drink that keeps the world go round.

- `L`

  The `l` has three pronounciations:

  1. like in the english word "left" on example word would be "letali≈°ƒçe" which w means airport [üì¢](https://forvo.com/word/letali%C5%A1%C4%8De/#sl).
  2. Before consonants it is pronounced like a 'w' an example would be `volk` [üì¢](https://forvo.com/word/volk/#sl) which means wolf üê∫.
  3. At the end of words it is also pronounced like a 'w' an slovene example word would be `pol` [üì¢](https://forvo.com/word/pol/#sl) which means half ex. `pol litra piva` half a liter of üç∫.

- `Lj`

  Is pronounced like the the 'li' in the french pronounciation of "battalion", the sound is very uncommon in the english language and will need a bit of training. A slovene Term where Lj is used is "Ljubljana" [üì¢](https://forvo.com/word/ljubljana/#sl) the beautiful captial of slovenia.

- `M`
 
    Is pronounced like the the 'm' in "mom". The slovene word `malica` [üì¢](https://forvo.com/word/malica/#sl) meaning snack or lunch uses this sound.

- `N`

  The 'n' has two two pronunciations:

  1. normally like the n in "nothing" for instance in the slovene word `nogomet` which means soccer.
  2. It is in some regions of slovenia pronounced like a √± as in "Se√±or" before an i; a common trait among Slavs examples are `ni` [nyee] or "nitje" (niti) ‚ö°

- `Nj`

  The combination of n+j is pronounced like the 'n' in "news" examples would be   `konj` which means horse [üì¢](https://forvo.com/word/ljubljana/#sl).
   or `njiva` [üì¢](https://forvo.com/word/njiva/#sl). which means field.

- `P`

  Is pronounced like the the 'p' in "path". One example word would be `pot` [üì¢](https://forvo.com/word/ljubljana/#sl).meaning path or way.

- `R`
  The `r` has two slightly different ways of pronounciation, depending if a vowel follows the 'r' (`gora`, `rjav`) or if it stands between two consonants (`vrh`,`prst`)
  1. One is like the 'r' in trilled like spanish 'r' in "rico" one example slovene word would be `riba` [üì¢](https://forvo.com/word/riba/#sl)
  2. The second is like a 'er' when used as a vowel, i.e. there is no other vowel after the 'r' examples would be `Trst` (The city of Trieste [üì¢](https://forvo.com/word/trst/#sl)  ), `grm` (Bush) or `vrt` (Garden [üì¢](https://forvo.com/search/vrt/sl/))

- `S`
    Is pronounced like the 's' in "seven", an example slovene word would be `sadje` meaning fruits [üì¢](https://forvo.com/word/sadje/#sl)

- `≈†`
  The letter ≈° is pronounced like the 'sh' in "shock" or "Shrek", a slovene example is "≈°est" (six)

- `T`

  The 't' is pronounced like in the english word "taxi" a slovene word using the `t` is `tovari≈°` (comrade [üì¢](https://forvo.com/word/tovari%C5%A1/#sl))

- `V`
  The letter 'v' can also be pronounced in two ways:
  1. Before a vowel it is pronounced like a `w`, slovene examples would be `voda` [üì¢](https://forvo.com/word/voda/#sl) or `vino` [üì¢](https://forvo.com/search/vino/sl/).
  2. After a vowel and at the end of words it is pronounced like a short ‚Äòw‚Äô glide examples are `avto` (car [üì¢](https://forvo.com/word/avto/#sl)), `ovca` (sheep [üì¢](https://forvo.com/word/ovca/#sl))
  3. At the start of a syllable before a consonant is is pronunced like the 'oo' in the english expression `oops`, common slovene word combinations are vs-, vƒç-, v≈°-‚Ä¶: `vse` (all [üì¢](https://forvo.com/word/vse/#sl)), `vƒçeraj` (yesterday [üì¢](https://forvo.com/word/v%C4%8Deraj/#sl))and `v≈°eƒç` (to like [üì¢](https://forvo.com/word/v%C5%A1e%C4%8D_si_mi/#sl))

- `Z`
  The 'z' si pronounced like in "zero", a slovene word would be "zlato" (gold) (See the story of zlato rog above) [üì¢](https://forvo.com/search/zlato/sl/).

- `≈Ω`
  The `≈Ω` is pronounced like the english 'su' in "pleasure" or 'ge' in "garage" a slovene example would be `≈ærtev` [üì¢](https://forvo.com/word/%C5%BErtev/#sl) (victim)
